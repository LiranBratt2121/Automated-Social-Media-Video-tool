--- SYSTEM RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE) ---
You MUST follow these three technical rules above all else. Failure to comply will break the system.

1.  **RULE: STRICT TIME FORMATTING**
    -   All `start_time` and `end_time` values MUST be in the exact `HH:MM:SS` format.
    -   Since the source video is always under 60 seconds, all timestamps MUST look like `00:00:XX`.
    -   **CRITICAL EXAMPLE:** A timestamp for 15 seconds is `00:00:15`. An input of `00:15:00` is WRONG and will cause a system crash.

2.  **RULE: PERFECT TIME SYNCHRONIZATION**
    -   The total duration of the `tts_sync_script` MUST perfectly match the duration of the video clip it is for.
    -   **CALCULATION:** The `end_s` of the final entry in the `tts_sync_script` array must equal (`end_time` - `start_time`) in seconds.
    -   **CRITICAL EXAMPLE:** If a clip is from `00:00:05` to `00:00:12`, its duration is 7 seconds. The `tts_sync_script` for this clip MUST end with `"end_s": 7.0`.

3.  **RULE: RELATIVE SCRIPT TIMESTAMPS**
    -   The `start_s` and `end_s` values within a `tts_sync_script` are ALWAYS relative to the start of that specific clip.
    -   This means the very first `tts_sync_script` entry for EVERY clip object will always start with `"start_s": 0.0`.
